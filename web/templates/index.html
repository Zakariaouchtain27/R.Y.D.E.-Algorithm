PRISM ENGINE

Not just price alerts.
Optimal stopping.

Most tools alert you when any price drops. PRISM runs 5,000 Monte Carlo price path simulations using Longstaff-Schwartz backward induction to answer a harder question:

“Given this price right now, will waiting likely produce a better one — or is this as good as it gets?”

Only when PRISM is statistically confident a fare is worth taking does it fire a STRIKE webhook. Fewer false alarms. More meaningful signals.

// Webhook payload on STRIKE

{
  "event": "ryde.decision",
  "action": "STRIKE",
  "booking_id": "bkg_abc123",
  "current_price": 530.00,
  "original_price": 850.00,
  "net_savings": 320.00,
  "confidence_score": 0.87,
  "seats_remaining": 3
}

Questions

Do you access our GDS or airline systems?

No. RYDE is signal-only. You push fare prices to us via PATCH requests, PRISM evaluates them, and we fire a webhook when you should act. Your agency executes the rebook in your own GDS. We never touch your reservation system.

What is a PRISM signal?

A webhook we send to your system when PRISM's Monte Carlo analysis determines a current fare is worth rebooking (STRIKE) or likely to fall further (PHANTOM_HOLD). Both carry a confidence score so you can filter by threshold.

How do I send fare prices to RYDE?

Send a PATCH to /v1/bookings/{id} with current_price and seats_remaining. Call this whenever your fare search results update. See the API docs.

What happens if my subscription lapses?

Your API key is suspended immediately. Monitoring stops and no webhooks are fired. Resume your subscription and the key reactivates automatically within seconds.

Can I test before committing to a paid plan?

Yes. The free plan includes 5 bookings with full PRISM access — no credit card required. It's the same API as paid plans, just capped at 5 concurrent monitored bookings.
